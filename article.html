On the pleasure of slow mornings

There is something about waking without an alarm that changes the texture of a whole day. The hours feel longer, not in a tedious way, but in the way a good meal feels longer than a rushed one.

I have been trying to protect my mornings lately. No phone for the first hour. Just coffee, the window, whatever book is on the table. It is a small discipline but it has made everything else easier to bear.

The world does not need me at seven in the morning. It barely needs me at nine. There is freedom in admitting that.
